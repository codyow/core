Section Initiatives & Programs

BLS is committed to encouraging diversity in the Section by fostering a welcoming environment for all lawyers and promoting full and equal participation by all lawyers, including lawyers of color, women lawyers, LGBTQ+ lawyers, and lawyers with disabilities. This commitment is advanced through annual initiatives, special programming, and ongoing diversity guidelines.

INITIATIVES
Diversity Clerkship Program

The Diversity Clerkship Program provides business law clerkship placements for qualified diverse second year law students each summer. Participating clerks receive support and mentoring in the business law field and exposure to business practices. In addition to a placement in a business court clerkship, participants also receive a stipend of $3,000 to cover living expenses for the duration of the 8-week clerkship.

Business Law Fellows Program

Each year, the Business Law Section appoints up to 12 diverse, outstanding lawyers to participate in two-year terms as Business Law Section Fellows. The program is designed to springboard participants into leadership roles within the Section by providing mentors, travel subsidies, and involvement opportunities. Apply for the 2021-2023 Business Law Fellows Class by May 7, 2021.

Jean Allard Glass Cutter

The Jean Allard Glass Cutter Award is presented annually to an exceptional woman business lawyer who has made significant contributions to the profession and the Business Law Section. The 2021 Glass Cutter Award recipient will be revealed during the Section Keynote Address and Awards Ceremony on Thursday, April 22 at 3:30 PM CDT.

PROGRAMMING
Programs & Diversity

BLS aims to create an environment that fosters and embraces diversity and inclusion throughout Section programming. CLE program panels are planned to reflect the diversity of the ABA with mandatory diversity requirements in mind.

Diversity and Inclusion Programming

View committee meetings and CLE programs focused on diversity at the Virtual Spring Meeting as identified by the Diversity and Inclusion Committee using the Diversity filter.

The Diversity and Inclusion Committee leads the Section's efforts to recruit and retain lawyers of color, women lawyers, lawyers with disabilities, gay, lesbian, bisexual and transgender lawyers, young lawyers, and law students ("Diverse Lawyers") for active involvement in the work and leadership of the Section.
